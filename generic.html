October 2025

Poster Presentation – IOE Prospective Student Workshop 2025

University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

Our IOE 591 (W25) project team was selected to present our research at the 2025 IOE Prospective Student Workshop, where exemplary projects were showcased to prospective graduate students, faculty, and staff. The team consisted of myself, Sanjay Kandaswamy, and Yuhan Jiang, guided by Professor Raymond Muscat. We presented our data-driven analysis of the Children’s Emergency Services bedside handoff process, combining workflow observations, time studies, machine learning for wait-time prediction, and discrete-event simulation to evaluate key operational delays. Using insights from 35,000+ patient records and simulation models built with Gamma, Exponential, and Lognormal distributions, we identified several opportunities for improvement. These included standardizing communication during handoff, adding clear specialty indicators in MiChart, initiating coordinated clinical teams earlier, enhancing discharge planning, and implementing predictive wait-time tools to support dynamic staffing and resource management. Our analysis highlighted actionable strategies to reduce bottlenecks and improve overall patient flow efficiency across CES operations.

September 2025

2nd Place Poster Award – IOE Research Symposium 2025

University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

Our team, the Reducing Emergency Department Crowding (REDC) group, earned 2nd place at the 2025 IOE Research Symposium for the poster “Optimizing CT Scan Operations in the Michigan Medicine Emergency Department.” The team consisted of Sandra Hawil, Grace Phan, Nethra Vijayakumar, Laney Hoving, Aimeng Yang, and myself, working under the guidance of Professor Amy Cohn and Vincenzo Loffredo. We developed a Python-based discrete-event simulation framework integrating queueing models, stochastic scenarios, and scanner utilization visualizations to replicate real-world CT workflows. Through over 100 simulation runs, we identified opportunities to free approximately 24 hours of daily bed capacity, providing actionable recommendations to hospital leadership. Building on this recognition, the project is now being expanded to examine additional bottlenecks in the Emergency Department.

February 2023

Led VVPAT Production for India’s 2024 Parliamentary Elections – Successfully Delivered 124,000 Units

Bharat Electronics Limited, Bangalore

I took on the role of Project Lead for the production of Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail (VVPAT) devices, a critical sub-project of the Electronic Voting Machine (EVM) program for India’s 2024 Parliamentary Elections. The target was to produce 124,000 VVPAT units, and I was responsible for procurement, vendor coordination, and production planning while ensuring full compliance with Election Commission of India standards. Working closely with the Development and Engineering teams, I ensured that construction and assembly followed approved specifications. I also managed E-Way Bills for material transport and maintained accurate production and logistics records in SAP. Through strong coordination and the combined efforts of our team, we successfully met production goals on time, contributing to the smooth execution of a nationwide election process.
