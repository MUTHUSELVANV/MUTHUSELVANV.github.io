Reducing Emergency Department Crowd (REDC)❤️

During my internship, I had the privilege of working with the Reducing Emergency Department Crowding (REDC) team, a talented group of teammates: Sandra, Grace, Nethra, Laney, and Aimeng, under the guidance of Amy Cohn and Vincenzo Loffredo. The team’s diverse backgrounds and skills made our collaboration both enjoyable and impactful.

Together, we developed a Python-based discrete-event simulation framework to evaluate Emergency Department CT workflows. By combining our expertise, we integrated queueing models, stochastic scenarios, and utilization visualizations to closely replicate real-world operations. Over the course of more than 100 simulation runs, we examined the effects of scanner downtime, process delays, and acuity-based patient prioritization strategies on system throughput and efficiency.

Our collective work identified opportunities to free up approximately 24 hours of daily bed capacity, providing hospital leadership with actionable recommendations to strengthen system resilience, improve resource allocation, and enhance patient flow. It was a privilege to contribute alongside such a lovely and talented team.

Jan 2025 – Apr 2025

Project Consultant
Michigan Medicine
University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, MI, USA

Children’s Emergency Services (CES) Improvement Initiative

Our IOE 591 project team, which included myself, Sanjay Kandaswamy, and Yuhan Jiang under the guidance of Professor Raymond Muscat, set out to understand why pediatric admissions in the Children’s Emergency Services at Michigan Medicine were often delayed. We began by mapping the handoff and admission workflow, speaking with staff involved in the process, and collecting detailed time study data. These steps helped us see where communication breakdowns and coordination gaps were slowing the movement of patients. To quantify these issues, we analyzed more than 35,000 patient records and built an XGBoost predictive model that estimated patient wait times. This helped us understand how wait times changed with arrival patterns and how staffing decisions could adapt more effectively. We then created a ProModel discrete-event simulation that used Gamma, Exponential, and Lognormal distributions to represent real patient flow, callbacks, transfers, and length of stay. The simulation allowed us to test how the system performed under different resource constraints. Using insights from these models, we proposed improvements such as introducing a discharge lounge, creating more standardized admission procedures, and building predictive dashboards to give teams better visibility. Our recommendations showed the potential to reduce average wait times by 11 percent and support a smoother and more resilient admission process.

Tools: Python, Tableau, Microsoft Excel
