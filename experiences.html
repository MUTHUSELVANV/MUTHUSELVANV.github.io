May 2025 – Aug 2025

Systems Engineering Research Intern
Center for Healthcare Engineering & Patient Safety (CHEPS)
University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, MI, USA

Reducing Emergency Department Crowd (REDC)❤️

During my internship, I had the privilege of working with the Reducing Emergency Department Crowding (REDC) team, a talented group including Sandra, Grace, Nethra, Laney, and Aimeng, under the guidance of Amy and Vincenzo. The team brought together diverse backgrounds and skills, which made working together both enjoyable and impactful.

Together, we developed a Python-based discrete-event simulation framework to evaluate Emergency Department CT workflows. By combining our expertise, we integrated queueing models, stochastic scenarios, and utilization visualizations to closely replicate real-world operations. Over the course of more than 100 simulation runs, we examined the effects of scanner downtime, delays, and acuity-based patient prioritization strategies on system throughput and efficiency.

Our collective work identified opportunities to free up approximately 24 hours of daily bed capacity, providing hospital leadership with actionable recommendations to strengthen resilience, improve resource allocation, and enhance patient flow. It was a privilege to contribute alongside such a lovely and talented team.

Tools: Python, Tableau, Microsoft Excel

Michigan Medicine
University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, MI, USA

Children’s Emergency Services (CES) Improvement Initiative

Conducted process mapping, stakeholder interviews, and detailed time studies to evaluate and improve pediatric admission efficiency in the Emergency Department. Designed a predictive machine learning model using XGBoost to estimate patient wait times, enabling data-driven and dynamic staffing decisions. Developed a discrete-event simulation framework to model patient flow and assess system performance under varying resource constraints. Through these analyses, proposed high-impact solutions such as implementing discharge lounge usage, standardizing admission procedures, and developing predictive dashboards. These recommendations demonstrated the potential to reduce average patient wait times by 11% while enhancing care flow efficiency and overall system resilience.
